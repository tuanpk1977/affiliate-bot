# EEAT Engine Design

## Objective

The EEAT engine should provide a reusable, documentation-first framework for strengthening author credibility, evidence quality, transparency, and user trust in generated content. It is designed to support review-style, comparison-style, and buying-guide-style articles without introducing implementation work.

This document is planning-only. No code, no deployment, and no publishing are included.

---

## 1. Purpose of the EEAT engine

The engine should help content pages communicate:

- who authored or reviewed the content
- how the product or topic was evaluated
- what evidence supports the claims
- where the content may be limited
- how affiliate relationships are handled
- how recent updates are reflected

The goal is to improve trust signals so that the page feels more credible and more useful to both readers and search engines.

---

## 2. Reusable EEAT structure

The EEAT engine should produce a standardized trust block that can be reused across article types.

### Core reusable components

- Author profile
- Testing methodology
- Pros and cons
- Evidence
- Limitations
- Comparison method
- Disclosure
- Affiliate disclosure
- Last updated
- Product testing notes
- Real experience block
- Transparency rules

These components should be assembled into a consistent trust framework for each article.

---

## 3. Author profile

### Purpose

The author profile gives readers context about who is evaluating the subject and why they are qualified to do so.

### What it should include

- author name or editorial identity
- role or title
- relevant expertise
- topic familiarity
- review or testing experience
- editorial standards

### Design guidance

The author profile should be factual, concise, and relevant to the topic. It should not feel fabricated or overly promotional.

### Output structure

- Author name
- Experience summary
- Editorial basis
- Review context

---

## 4. Testing methodology

### Purpose

The testing methodology explains how the product, tool, or topic was evaluated.

### What it should describe

- what was tested
- how it was tested
- how long it was tested
- what conditions were used
- what criteria mattered most
- whether testing was hands-on or research-based

### Design guidance

The methodology should be specific enough to feel credible but not so technical that it becomes hard to read.

### Output structure

- Test subject
- Test duration
- Evaluation criteria
- Environment or use case
- Result summary

---

## 5. Pros and cons

### Purpose

A balanced pros and cons section improves honesty and usefulness.

### Design guidance

The engine should encourage a fair and transparent evaluation rather than a one-sided endorsement.

### Output structure

- Pros
- Cons
- Best-fit audience
- Best-use scenarios
- Tradeoffs

### Rules

- Pros and cons should be specific.
- They should be grounded in the article’s evidence.
- They should not be generic filler.

---

## 6. Evidence

### Purpose

Evidence makes claims stronger and more believable.

### Evidence types

- observed experience
- documented features
- pricing or plan details
- screenshots or examples
- known limitations
- comparison data
- external references when relevant

### Design guidance

The article should clearly distinguish between:

- direct observation
- secondary research
- inferred conclusions

### Output structure

- Evidence point
- Source type
- Strength of evidence
- Relevance to claim

---

## 7. Limitations

### Purpose

Limitations improve credibility by showing where the information may be incomplete or context-dependent.

### What to include

- scope limits
- time-based limitations
- audience-specific constraints
- pricing or plan variability
- availability or regional differences
- lack of long-term testing

### Design guidance

Limitations should be framed as practical context, not as a weakness. They should help the reader make a better decision.

### Output structure

- Limitation
- Why it matters
- What readers should consider

---

## 8. Comparison method

### Purpose

For comparison content, the engine should explain how the comparison was made.

### What it should describe

- what criteria were used
- why those criteria matter
- how the options were rated
- whether weightings were applied
- whether the comparison is general or use-case specific

### Design guidance

The comparison method should be transparent enough that readers can understand the basis of the conclusion.

### Output structure

- Comparison criteria
- Evaluation method
- Weighting approach
- Reader fit guidance

---

## 9. Disclosure

### Purpose

Disclosure helps readers understand whether the content includes sponsored, affiliate, or commercial relationships.

### What it should include

- whether the content includes affiliate links
- whether compensation may be received
- whether the review is editorial or sponsored
- whether recommendations are influenced by commercial relationships

### Design guidance

Disclosure should be clear, direct, and easy to find.

### Output structure

- Disclosure statement
- Relationship status
- Commercial impact summary

---

## 10. Affiliate disclosure

### Purpose

Affiliate disclosure should be handled separately from general disclosure because it affects trust and compliance.

### What it should include

- that the site may earn commissions
- that this does not change the editorial recommendation
- that the page remains focused on usefulness and honesty

### Design guidance

The affiliate disclosure should be plain-language, non-awkward, and easy to understand.

### Output structure

- Affiliate disclosure sentence
- Commission explanation
- Editorial independence statement

---

## 11. Last updated

### Purpose

A visible last-updated signal helps readers understand whether the content is current.

### What it should reflect

- the date of review
- whether the content was revised recently
- whether pricing, features, or guidance changed

### Design guidance

The update date should be included wherever it strengthens freshness and confidence.

### Output structure

- Last updated date
- Revision summary
- Freshness note

---

## 12. Product testing notes

### Purpose

Product testing notes make the evaluation feel concrete and grounded.

### What they should describe

- what was tried
- what happened during testing
- whether the experience was smooth or frustrating
- whether outcomes were consistent
- whether certain workflows were tested more than others

### Design guidance

Testing notes should be practical and evidence-based rather than purely promotional or purely technical.

### Output structure

- Test scenario
- Observed result
- Key takeaway
- Relevance to the reader

---

## 13. Real experience block

### Purpose

A real experience block helps the article feel lived-in and trustworthy.

### What it should include

- direct observations
- specific use cases
- practical notes
- context about the experience
- a realistic summary of what users may expect

### Design guidance

This block should feel honest and useful. It should not become anecdotal filler or a personal diary entry.

### Output structure

- Experience summary
- Observed outcome
- Practical insight
- Reader takeaway

---

## 14. Transparency rules

### Purpose

The EEAT engine should enforce a consistent standard for trust and honesty.

### Transparency rules

- Do not overstate certainty.
- Distinguish between first-hand experience and external research.
- Clearly label sponsored or affiliate relationships.
- Show both strengths and weaknesses.
- Do not present opinion as fact.
- Do not claim features or performance without evidence.
- Make limitations visible.
- Use plain and direct language.

### Editorial principle

The content should feel trustworthy because it is clear, balanced, and grounded in evidence.

---

## 15. Reusable EEAT output format

The engine should produce a reusable trust package that can be inserted into articles in a consistent format.

### Suggested output sections

1. Author profile
2. Testing methodology
3. Pros and cons
4. Evidence
5. Limitations
6. Comparison method
7. Disclosure
8. Affiliate disclosure
9. Last updated
10. Product testing notes
11. Real experience block
12. Transparency summary

---

## 16. Expected outcome

A well-designed EEAT engine should help generated content feel more credible, more transparent, and more useful by making trust signals explicit rather than implicit. This should improve reader confidence and strengthen the quality of review, comparison, and buying-guide content.
